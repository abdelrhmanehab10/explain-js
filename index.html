What are Event Bubbling and Capturing?

When an event occurs on an element, it first runs the handlers on it, then on its parent, then all the way up on other ancestors. This is called bubbling.

The capturing phase is the opposite of bubbling. The event first triggers on the outermost ancestor, then moves inward to the target element.
